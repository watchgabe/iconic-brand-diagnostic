01 — One-of-one lane

Define the lane only you can credibly own.

Not your niche. Your lane is the intersection of taste, lived experience, and the result you produce. The thing a competitor literally could not say without lying.

  • One sentence: "I help [specific person] get [specific outcome] through [specific lens only you have]."
  • Three things you do that the obvious competitors will not.
  • The opinion you'd defend on a podcast even if it cost you a client.
02 — Avatar + AI Brain inputs

Write the real person, not the demographic.

This becomes the source of truth for every hook, caption, and script. Be specific to the point of discomfort.

  • Their actual day, hour by hour.
  • The real desire they wouldn't admit at a dinner party.
  • What they've already tried that didn't work.
  • The thought that hits them at 11pm.
  • The 12-month dream they're quietly chasing.
  • Drop all of the above into your AI Brain alongside your origin story, offer, and voice.
03 — Visual identity decisions

Lock the look so someone can ID your post with the username covered.

  • Signature color (one, not a palette). Hex code committed.
  • Two fonts: one for display, one for body. Picked, not maybe.
  • Color grade rule for photo/video. One look, applied every time.
  • Settings, light, and composition you'll actually return to. Cinematic means intentional, not expensive.
04 — Content capture + batch system

Volume without dropping the bar requires infrastructure.

  • Capture: where ideas land the moment they happen (voice memos, one note, one inbox).
  • Pillars: three to five themes everything maps back to.
  • Batch days: when scripts get written, when shoots happen, when edits ship.
  • Target: five short-form per week, one to two long-form per week. Build to that rate, don't start there.
05 — Quality floor checklist

Each piece is a sample of your taste. Set the floor, then never go under it.

  • Hook earns the watch in the first two seconds.
  • Frame, light, and audio meet your visual identity.
  • One idea per piece. No filler.
  • Caption holds up if read on its own.
  • If you wouldn't pin it to your profile, don't post it.
